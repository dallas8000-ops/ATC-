## Example 12: Complex Clearance

### BEFORE (Incorrect):
"American 185, you're cleared to Chicago O'Hare via WYNDE3 departure, Rwy 28C, climb & maintain 5,000, expect FL350 10 min after departure, freq. 124.9, squawk 4521."

### AFTER (Correct):
AMERICAN ONE EIGHT FIVE, you are cleared to CHICAGO OHARE via WYNDE THREE departure, runway two eight CHARLIE, climb and maintain five thousand, expect flight level three five zero one zero minutes after departure, frequency one two four point nine, squawk four five two one

### Violations:
- ⚠ Quotation marks (Image 3)
- ⚠ Multiple periods (Image 3)
- ⚠ Numbers as digits (Images 6-7)
- ⚠ "Rwy" abbreviation
- ⚠ "Runway" capitalized (Image 5)
- ⚠ "&" symbol instead of "and"


## Common Mistakes to Avoid (From Training Images):

### Image 1: Most Common Failures
1. ❌ Formatting violations (highest impact on pass/fail)
2. ❌ Callsign handling errors
3. ❌ NATO phonetic spelling errors
4. ❌ Number transcription errors
5. ❌ Not including filler words
6. ❌ Skipped or incomplete work

### Image 3: Punctuation Errors
1. ❌ Using periods (.)
2. ❌ Using question marks (?)
3. ❌ Using quotation marks (" or ')
4. ✅ ONLY commas are allowed

### Images 4-5: Capitalization Errors
1. ❌ Capitalizing "One" at start of sentence
2. ❌ Capitalizing "Flight Level"
3. ❌ Capitalizing "Runway", "Airport", "Tower", "Ground"
4. ❌ Partial callsign capitalization ("Volaris" instead of "VOLARIS")
5. ✅ Entire callsign in ALL CAPS

### Images 6-7: Number Errors
1. ❌ Writing digits (123 instead of one two three)
2. ❌ Using hyphens (12-34 or TWENTY-NINE)
3. ❌ Not spelling out every digit
4. ❌ Grouping numbers (ONE SEVEN instead of SEVENTEEN)
5. ✅ Spell each digit separately

### Image 8: Filler Word Errors
1. ❌ Removing filler words (uh, um, oh, ah, hmm)
2. ✅ Include ALL audible filler words

### Images 9-10: Bracket Errors
1. ❌ Leaving empty brackets <>
2. ❌ Using parentheses () instead of angled brackets
3. ❌ Using square brackets []
4. ❌ Using ellipsis (...)
5. ✅ Always use <best guess> with angled brackets

### Image 11: Callsign Errors
1. ❌ Partial capitalization ("United" or "volaris")
2. ❌ Lowercase numbers in callsign ("seventeen" instead of "SEVENTEEN")
3. ✅ ENTIRE callsign in ALL CAPS


## Tips for Good Transcription:

### Based on Image 12 (How to Identify Callsigns):
1. ✅ **Listen for Placement:** Callsigns typically at beginning/end of transmission
2. ✅ **Listen for Patterns:** Word/telephony + numbers + phonetic letters
3. ✅ **Listen for Controllers:** They address aircraft by callsign before instructions

### General Best Practices:
1. ✅ Listen multiple times if needed
2. ✅ Use angled brackets <> when uncertain (never empty)
3. ✅ Spell out ALL numbers completely
4. ✅ Identify callsigns at start of transmissions
5. ✅ Include all filler words you hear
6. ✅ Use commas to separate phrases naturally
7. ✅ Keep non-callsign words lowercase
8. ✅ Double-check with the auto-formatter
9. ✅ Review violations tab for specific errors
